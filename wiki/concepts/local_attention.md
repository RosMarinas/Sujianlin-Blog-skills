# Local Attention

Local attention is a mechanism where tokens primarily attend to their nearby neighbors rather than all tokens in a sequence. It acts as an inductive bias that is highly effective for language models, significantly aiding in length extrapolation. Several positional penalties (like ALIBI, KERPLE) effectively simulate local attention.
